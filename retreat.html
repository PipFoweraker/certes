ARCTas

Alignment, Resilience, Community — Tasmania
Late Summer Research Retreat | Feb–Mar 2026

Note: This retreat is by invitation only and is not open for general registration. This page provides information for potential donors and partners about our planned research activities. If you believe you should have received an invitation, please contact us.

Building Infrastructure for Alignment Research Continuity

Some civilisational disruption scenarios that may arise in the lead up to the advent of superintelligence are severe enough to halt most AI research activity, but not so severe that completing formal alignment work becomes irrelevant. In those scenarios—where alignment research still matters, and can even capitalise on an unexpected reprieve in capabilities gains, but most research environments fail—having geographic redundancy becomes critical.

Currently, alignment research capacity is concentrated in a few locations, creating fragility we think needs addressing.

ARCTas is our response to this: a resilient research community in Tasmania that can support alignment work even through major, prolonged disruption. We model Tasmania's geographic position, resource stability, and distance from likely disruption zones as significant advantages. Beyond disruption scenarios, we're also building for research excellence under normal conditions—an environment genuinely optimized for the work rather than for proximity to existing infrastructure.

We are currently setting up ARCTas. This retreat is our prototype. We're testing what enables excellent alignment research within a resilient community.

What to Expect

You may choose to join us for the core 2-week retreat or to prolong your stay by another 2 weeks. Both formats emphasize substantive collaboration with protected work time:

2-Week Format

Intensive model exchange and collaborative exploration. Opening days use structured discussion to surface everyone's current thinking and identify promising directions. From there, participating researchers will explore perspectives on large-scale strategy, formal/agent foundations-based superintelligence alignment, and pathways towards success.

4-Week Format

Model-sharing followed by sustained project work, with extended blocks for deep collaboration or individual research. We expect this format will more likely yield tangible research output.

If this environment works well for you, longer residencies will become possible as ARCTas develops.

The setting: Tasmania in late summer. You'd work in a countryside location designed for focus—natural light, open views, the kind of environment where thoughts clarify. Remote collaboration infrastructure keeps you connected (9 am in Tasmania = 3 pm in California).

Running alongside the research: optional relational practices, evening activities/campfires, local excursions. We think it's plausible that engaging with these may enhance research clarity, surface unexpected insights, and resource you physically and psychologically, but you're welcome to participate as much or as little as suits you. The rhythm is yours to set.

Participant Groups

We're currently inviting selected researchers.

While the retreat is designed to support your ability to do exceptional work, it will bring together not only alignment researchers, but also two other groups of people who bring expertise in building/running crucial parts of ARCTas' physical and social infrastructure:

  • Tasmanian permaculture experts
  • A team of socio- and psychotechnology experts

At the retreat, you will encounter their work directly—you'll see them building and testing models of what makes communities like this prototype resilient and individuals thrive. They'll be focusing on their own work while you do yours. Your research work and your natural interactions with others—conversations over lunch, walks when you want fresh air, optional evening activities, days out—help us all understand what actually matters to support excellent research and design the social and physical environment we're building accordingly.

These questions inform the retreat design:

  • How to create the optimal environment for high-quality, focused research work; and
  • How to thrive across three different cultures, cohering as a community that can weather disruption.

About Us

We are plex, Phil Chen, Pip Foweraker and Matilda da Rui, co-founders of ARCTas. We share a deep commitment to the survival and flourishing of life, a keen awareness that powerful AI built without robust alignment may end all life, and a combined wealth of knowledge spanning technical AI safety and the AI safety research and funding ecosystem, civilisational resilience, socio- and psychotechnologies, organisational administration, governance and culture, operations, community infrastructure and more.
